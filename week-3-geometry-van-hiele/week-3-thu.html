Week 3 · Thursday — Clinic

Geometry, 2.G / 3.G attributes · Poser · listening for the level
Today's goals: Put a name to Tuesday's spread — van Hiele levels — and learn to hear the level in how a learner reasons, not just what they name. The aim is to locate, not to rank: you diagnose in order to offer the right next experience.

1 · Name the levels (~20 min)

  • Debrief the readings — bring your Week 3 Reading Homework — then put the levels on the board: Level 0 names by appearance ("a diamond"), Level 1 reasons from properties ("equal sides, straight, closed"), Level 2 relates them ("a square is a special rectangle").
  • Today's goal: connect the routines you ran Tuesday to these levels — the same spread, now with a name.

Purpose: Tuesday's spread wasn't error — it was level. Naming it is the first diagnostic move.

2 · Run the nVoke diagnostic together (~20 min)

  • As a class, open nVoke and run the van Hiele Geometry pack in Diagnostic mode. Each of you types what you see for every figure and keeps your own responses.
  • Field every question about the app now, so it's second nature when you pose it yourself.

Purpose: the class becomes one dataset. nVoke shows the responses and does no analysis — you do the diagnosing.

3 · Locate the level (~20 min)

  • Read your own responses through the levels — where did you land? (You won't all be Level 2.)
  • Swap with a partner and locate theirs from the reasoning, not the label. Locate, don't rank — a "diamond" answer is data, not an error.

Purpose: hearing the level in the reasoning is the whole skill — the pick tells you little, the How do you know? is the diagnosis.

4 · Open the assignment + wrap (~20 min)

  • We open Pedagogical Excursion #3 together and read through it — that's the one place the details live.
  • Close: how did naming the levels change what you heard?

If you finish early

  • Take a response you'd call Level 0 and write the one question you'd ask to invite the next level — without telling.
Wrap-up & looking ahead:
  • This week's collected work: Pedagogical Excursion #3 and your Week 3 Reading Homework.
