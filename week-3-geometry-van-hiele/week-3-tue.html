Week 3 · Tuesday — Studio

Geometry, 2.G / 3.G attributes · Poser · the same figure, seen different ways
Today's goals: Debrief what genAI pushed you on from last week, then live three What Do You See? / How Do You Know? geometry talks. Feel how one figure gets seen many ways — "diamond," "rhombus," "parallelogram" — and that the spread isn't error, it's real. We name what it is on Thursday; today you just live it.

1 · Debrief last week's genAI feedback (~20 min)

  • You ran your Week 2 MQR rationale past genAI. What did it push you on — was your reasoning specific to that child, or too general?
  • Would you revise your question? Where did genAI help, and where did it miss?

Purpose: carry the questioning stance forward — from critiquing your own rationale to reading how others see.

2 · Three geometry talks — you present (~60 min · ~20 each)

  • We run each routine together — flash a figure, "What do you see?", collect a few, then "How do you know?" Ask, never tell.
  • WODB Which One Doesn't Belong? — four figures; pick one that doesn't belong and defend it (every figure can be defended).
  • Same & Different — two or three figures; say what's the same and what's different.
  • Sort — a handful of figures; group them and state your rule.

Purpose: three routines keep the demand varied, but all surface the same thing — how differently people see the same form.

If you finish early

  • Take one figure we ran and find a reason to include it that no one gave. How many honestly-different "sees" can one figure hold?
Wrap-up & looking ahead:
