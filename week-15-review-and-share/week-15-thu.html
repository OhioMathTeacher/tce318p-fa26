Week 15 · Thursday — Potluck, Share & Debrief

Act III · the last class · share what you're proud of, then talk shop about the paper
Today's goals: The last class. Bring something to share. Each of us shares one piece of our writing we're proud of, then we talk through where drafts stand and how Todd-in-a-Can worked as a reviewer — which feeds your Review & Reflection.

1 · Potluck & welcome (~10 min)

  • Settle in and share food. One semester ago you met a concept and a student's thinking about it; today we close the loop together.

Purpose: this work has been close and personal. We finish it as a community of teachers.

2 · Share a piece you're proud of (~40 min)

  • Share one piece of your draft you're genuinely proud of — a paragraph, a figure and your reading of it, a turn in your analysis — and say why it's the piece you'd point to.
  • As audience: notice what makes a piece land. You'll carry those moves back into your own draft.

Purpose: naming your own best work — and hearing others' — is how you learn what strong evidence-based writing sounds like.

3 · Drafts & Todd-in-a-Can (~25 min)

  • Where do drafts stand? Bring your open questions about the paper and we'll work them out together.
  • Debrief the AI reviewer: what did Todd-in-a-Can catch, what did it miss, what did you change because of it — and what did you reject, and why? How well did it actually work?

Purpose: an honest read of the AI reviewer is part of the inquiry — you're learning when a machine read helps your thinking and when it doesn't.

Wrap-up:
  • Finish strong: your Final Report and your Review & Reflection are both due Dec 9 (two separate submissions). Thank you for a semester of looking closely.
