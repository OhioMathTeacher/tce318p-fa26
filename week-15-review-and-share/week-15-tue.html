Week 15 · Tuesday — Write & Peer Review

Act III · the last stretch · one human read before you finish
Today's goals: Bring your draft. First half is writing time. Second half, a colleague reads your draft and pushes on one thing — does your evidence support your claims? Todd-in-a-Can, our AI reviewer, is there whenever you have a draft — run it as often as it helps.

1 · Write (~35 min)

  • Protected writing. Pick up where you left off over the break and push the thin parts — often the literature (What Does Research Say?) and Next Steps, the sections hardest to draft alone.
  • The instructor circulates: a quick read, a question about a claim, a check that a figure is doing the work you say it is.

Purpose: a review is only as useful as the draft you bring it. Use this half to get your draft as whole as you can.

2 · Peer review (~35 min)

  • Trade drafts with a colleague and read the whole thing, but focus on the heart: your Collected Data and your tentative answer. One question drives the read: does the evidence actually support the claims?
  • Point to specifics: where a claim outruns its artifact, where a figure needs a caption or a closer read, where the answer is more confident than the evidence allows.

Purpose: a careful human reader catches where your evidence and your claims have drifted apart — while there's still time to fix it. This is the last human read before you finish.

Wrap-up & looking ahead:
  • Keep using Todd-in-a-Can as you revise — as often as it helps, and at least once before you submit. Thursday we talk about how it's been working.
  • Thursday is our last class: a potluck, a share, and a draft debrief. The Final Report is due Dec 9.
