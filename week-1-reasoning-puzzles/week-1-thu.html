Week 1 · Thursday — Clinic

Mathematics PK–5 · Learner · making your own thinking visible
Today's goals: Name what Tuesday was — figuring things out by reasoning, where the struggle was the point — then play all three games in the app, watching your own thinking with playback and mark-up. At the end we'll look at your first Pedagogical Excursion together.

1 · Debrief + reading (~25 min)

  • Back to Tuesday's puzzles: how did you figure out the rules? Where was the struggle?
  • In-class skim → think-pair-share of McFeetors & Palfy (2017) — bring your reading-homework entry (ranking the opening moves; competitive vs. solitaire).
  • Together, name what Tuesday was: inductive reasoning; the struggle was the point; process over answer.

Purpose: put language to the disposition you just lived — reasoning, not being told.

2 · Game 1 — Metadoku, together (~15 min)

  • We'll play Metadoku as a class to learn the app: playback (watch your own solve step by step), mark-up (note what you were thinking), and the report you can download.
  • Debrief: what did watching your own solve show you that you didn't notice while solving?

3 · Game 2 — Metaplete (~15 min)

  • Now on your own: play Metaplete, then play it back and mark up your solving.
  • Debrief: was your thinking different in this game than in Metadoku?

4 · Game 3 — Metagons (~15 min)

  • Same again with Metagons — play, play it back, mark it up.
  • Debrief: across all three games, where did you have a real strategy, and where were you guessing?

5 · Look at the assignment together (~10 min)

  • We'll open Pedagogical Excursion #1 and read through it together — that's the one place the details live.
  • You'll finish it at home and submit it by Sunday.

If you finish early

  • Look back at your three games — which one do you want to write up for the assignment, and why?
