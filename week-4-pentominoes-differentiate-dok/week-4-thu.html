Week 4 · Thursday — Clinic

Measurement & Data · designing tasks · Planner · build a question for every learner
Today's goals: Learn the Pentomino Builder through its three stages — Build, Pose, Play — and meet the differentiation dial in each. Across today you'll pose one task at each DOK level (four in all) and have a classmate play them. Bring your reading-journal entry on Edwards and Simpson. Today's work is Pedagogical Excursion #4.

1 · Name the frameworks (~10 min)

  • Debrief the readings you did since Tuesday — bring your reading-journal entry. From Edwards (argue it, don't just measure it) and Simpson (the Cognitive Rigor Matrix).
  • Put the matrix on the board: Bloom's × DOK. Bust the myth — differentiation ≠ easier numbers; often it's the same content at a different depth.

Purpose: Tuesday you named the depth dial (DOK); today the full matrix gives you the language to place any task — and three more dials waiting in the app.

2 · Building — ① Build the puzzle (~20 min)

  • Open the Pentomino Builder. Together we'll start with ① Build the puzzle — draw a frame, then place, rotate, flip, and color pieces; try the grid on/off toggle, the size control, and 1 Set vs Multi. Then take a few minutes to explore — poke at the other tabs too; that's how you learn the tool.
  • Build one real, solvable puzzle from the pieces — the placement you make is its answer key.
  • Differentiation dial — access: grid on scaffolds, grid off abstracts; a smaller frame lets more learners start. Same math, different way in.

Purpose: the build surface is where you differentiate access — who can pick up the task at all.

3 · Posing — ② Task & DOK (~20 min)

  • Together we'll walk through ② Task & DOK: write the prompt, pick a DOK level, and name who it's for — "for the learner who…" Then explore on your own.
  • Your target for today: one task at each DOK level — four in all (recall · apply · reason · design). Pose your first one now so you can play right away; build the rest as you go.
  • Differentiation dial — depth: the DOK level of the question, held apart from how hard the numbers are. This is the Cognitive Rigor Matrix, made concrete.

Purpose: posing is where you differentiate depth — the same pieces asked four ways, one per DOK level.

4 · Playing — ③ Play (~20 min)

  • Together we'll open ③ Play. Swap with a partner: a classmate plays your task as the learner it was posed for, and the app captures it in the play report.
  • A play only takes a few minutes — so go back and pose your next DOK level (① Build → ② Pose), then trade again. Keep the make ↔ play loop going until all four levels are built and played.
  • Differentiation dial — response: the play report — all four tasks with a classmate's play — is your evidence of how each task met its learner.

Purpose: playing turns your four tasks into evidence — the play report (all four levels) is what you submit for PE#4.

If you finish early

  • Take your DOK-1 task and rewrite the prompt to lift it to DOK-3 — same shape, deeper question.
