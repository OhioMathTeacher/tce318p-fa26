Week 5 · Tuesday — Studio

Assessment · rubrics · Assessor · one work sample, many rubrics
Today's goals: Explore rubrics as an assessor's lens. Score the same real student work several ways, watch the score move, and name what each rubric actually measures — the lens you'll one day point at a single real learner.

1 · Score it cold (~10 min)

  • On the handout: the NAEP "Amusement Park" task, four real student work samples (each got the numbers right), and the rubrics.
  • Score one sample 0–4, no rubric, just your gut. Put your number on the board.

Purpose: with no shared lens, our scores scatter — and a gut score hides what we were actually rewarding.

2 · Same samples, different rubrics — jigsaw (~30 min)

  • Your table is assigned one of the four rubrics on the handout — not all four. Score all four samples with it, and record each score and the reason it earned that score. Then we pool every table's scores into one grid.

Purpose: the rubric is the lens. Swap the lens and the "same" work looks different.

3 · Why did the score move? (~25 min)

  • Take one sample and read its scores straight across the grid. What do you notice?
  • Find the rubric that scored it highest and the one that scored it lowest — for the same work. What was each one looking at?
  • Do some of these rubrics work the same way as each other? Group them, and say what each group has in common.
  • Pick one rubric. Where does it spend its points? What does that tell you it cares about?

Purpose: these questions walk students to the pattern from the grid they built — we name it together, we don't announce it.

4 · Which lens shows the learner? (~15 min)

  • Which of these four rubrics would actually help you decide what to teach this child next — and which only hand you a grade?
  • If your goal is to see where a learner is — not just score them — which rubric would you reach for, and why?
  • That choice is the Assessor's move. Thursday you'll aim this lens at one real child — the field study begins.
Wrap-up & looking ahead:

  • Thursday: the field study, in one picture — how the whole back half of the course works, and how today's lens becomes the way you profile one real learner.
