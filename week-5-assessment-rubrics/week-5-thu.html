Week 5 · Thursday — Clinic

Assessment · the field study, in one picture · Assessor · judge one learner, then meet the project it opens
Today's goals: First, do the Assessor's move on your own — judge one real learner's work with a rubric and name the next teaching step. Then see the whole field study that this one lens opens: the back half of the course, anchored to one real child.

1 · Judge one learner — a Misconception Quick Response (~30 min)

  • This is your second MQR — the same quick, in-class, no-AI look at real student work you did back in Week 2, but this time you bring a rubric to it. It's collected today; the full prompt and how it's scored live in Pedagogical Excursion #5.
  • You get a fresh grade-4 item you did not see Tuesday — a geometry "how are these alike, and how are they different?" task (rectangle vs. parallelogram, a callback to the van Hiele week) — with one real, de-identified student response.
  • It takes the half hour because you do the Assessor's whole move: name the DOK the task asks for (a callback to Week 4), judge whether the child reached it and what they do and don't yet understand, then write one open, non-leading question — pitched at the right DOK — to move their thinking forward.

Purpose: Tuesday you explored the lens with your table. Now you point it yourself and commit — a score, a reason, and a next step. That commitment is the Assessor's real work.

2 · The field study, in one picture (~25 min)

  • What you just did — read where a learner is, with a lens — is how the back half of this course begins. It is one inquiry project, anchored to one real child, in four phases:
PhaseWhenWhat you do
Profilethe field visitYour host teacher nominates one focal learner. You learn the upcoming lesson and gather de-identified evidence of where that learner is — the move you rehearsed today.
Designafter the visitBuild a de-identified learning profile, then design tailored materials that supplement — not replace — the lesson the host is already teaching.
Implementthe immersionPut your materials in front of that learner and collect their work. The materials you designed are the instrument that gathers the data.
Analyze & sharethe closing weeksAnalyze how the learner responded, write it up as a case study, and share what you found.

Purpose: every role you have built this campus block — poser, planner, assessor — was practice for aiming all three at one real learner. This is where they meet.

3 · What makes it work — and how today connects (~13 min)

  • Supplement, don't supplant. You add one tailored layer for one child. It works even from a support role — you are not taking over the host's lesson.
  • The host nominates the learner. You do not pick; the classroom teacher does. It keeps the project bounded and ethical.
  • De-identification is not optional. The child is only ever a persona — no names. A real child's name and raw work stay in the classroom; they never go into a profile, a slide, or a shared file.
  • The through-line: the rubric you used today is the profiling instrument. Reading a learner with a lens is exactly how the project opens — you have already done it once.

If you finish the MQR early

  • Swap with a neighbor and read their questions. Which one best invites the student's own reasoning — without nudging them toward the answer? Say why.
