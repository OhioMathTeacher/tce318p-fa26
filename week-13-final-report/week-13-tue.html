1 · Welcome back (~10 min)

  • Two weeks in the field, running your Investigation Plan and collecting work. With a partner, then the room: what surprised you? What did you come back with — and what were you not able to get?

Purpose: the field is a blur until you say it out loud. Naming what you collected — and what you didn't — is where the analysis starts.

2 · Share-out — your question and one piece (~35 min)

  • Share two things: your guiding question (what you set out to learn) and one telling artifact you collected — plus a first read of what it shows about how your student(s) understood the concept.
  • The room helps you see it: what does this piece let you see? What's another way to read it? Is it evidence for your question, or next to it?
  • Keep notes on what colleagues surface about your piece — it feeds your Collected Data.

Purpose: a first human read of your evidence, out loud, before you write it up. "What did you learn?" starts here.

3 · Walk the Final Report together (~25 min)

  • We open the Final Report and read it together, section by section — what each section asks for, the format rules, and how it's graded. The spine: what did you seek to learn, and what did you learn?, answered with your own artifacts.
  • It's graded on evidence-based reasoning and honesty about limits — not on whether you "answered" your question. Bring your questions about the assignment now.

Purpose: knowing the shape of the paper before you write keeps Thursday about writing, not decoding the assignment.

Wrap-up & tonight's start:
