Week 13 · Thursday — Writing Day

Act III · the week back · start the paper — question first, then the evidence
Today's goals: A working writing session. Start the Final Report — set your guiding question at the top, then lay in your evidence and write what it shows. Bring your artifacts and the guidelines.

1 · Set your question at the top (~10 min)

  • Open a document and write, at the very top, your guiding question and the two it answers: what did I seek to learn? and what did I learn? Everything in the paper reports to those.

Purpose: the question is the spine. Written where you can see it, it keeps the draft from wandering.

2 · Write — section by section (~50 min)

  • Protected writing, in parts. Start with the heart — your Collected Data: drop in your telling figures and tables, label and caption each, and write what each shows about how your student(s) understood the concept (~30 min).
  • Then frame it: draft your Question and Methods around the evidence (~20 min). Leave What Does Research Say? and Next Steps for the break — you'll have more to say once the evidence is on the page.
  • The instructor circulates — a quick read of a paragraph, a question about a claim, a check that your figure is doing the work you say it is.

Purpose: writing one part at a time beats staring at the whole paper. Draft the evidence first, and every other section has something to point at.

3 · Trade a paragraph (~15 min)

  • Trade one Collected Data paragraph with a neighbor. One question for the reader: is the claim tied to the artifact — can you see in the figure what the writer says is there?

Purpose: a claim about thinking is only as good as the piece of work it points to. A second reader catches where the evidence and the claim have drifted apart.

Wrap-up & looking ahead:
  • Over the coming week and the break, push toward a full rough draft — all five parts down, rough is fine. Then Dec 1 we write and peer-review, Dec 3 is our potluck share, and the Final Report is due Dec 9.
