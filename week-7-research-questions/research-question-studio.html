2 · Your QFocus — the work you collected

Enter your Week 6 work samples one at a time — these are your QFocus, the thing your questions come from. Describe each one de-identified: the task, what the child did, and the strategy or error you noticed. Add a photo of the work and cover any names right on it. Keep going until you've entered them all.

Choosing good samples. Aim for 3–5 well-chosen samples — work collected recently, and/or tied to the math your student will study in the field (Weeks 11–12). Capture one work sample per image: a single page (like one worksheet), photographed or scanned so it is large and clear. One clear page is easier to read than an image that crowds several pages together.

3 · Brainstorm questions about your work samples

Look closely at the work you collected and produce as many questions as you can. (Flip back to Collected work anytime to see the images.)

Follow the four QFT rules:

  1. Ask as many questions as you can.
  2. Do not stop to discuss, judge, or answer any question.
  3. Write every question down exactly as it is thought.
  4. Turn any statement into a question.

Then mark each one O open (invites more than a yes/no) or C closed — you'll notice which kind you tend to ask.
