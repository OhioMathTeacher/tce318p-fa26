Week 7 · Tuesday — Studio

Act II · aim at my question · from field noticing to an inquiry · you were just in the classroom — what do you want to find out?
Today's goals: Make sense of what you saw in the field, and reconnect with the learner you already identified there — the mathematics you're setting out to understand, and the work you collected. That is the raw material you'll turn into a research question on Thursday.
Where this is heading — Weeks 7–10. Today's question starts one project: a single lesson you'll build to investigate it.
  • Before — a short routine that warms up the thinking.
  • During — a rich task that makes the child's understanding visible.
  • After — a culminating piece you can analyze: an exit slip, or a discussion you capture (photograph the board, or have students jot their reasoning). The one rule: come away with an artifact.
By Week 10 you'll have the full plan. In the field, enact as much as your placement allows — one student or a whole class, one session or several; you need not teach every part. The goal: gain insight into student thinking, collect the work that shows it, and report on it in your Week 15 Case Study.

1 · A new half of the course starts here (~5 min)

  • For five weeks you practiced the moves — Learner, Listener, Poser, Planner, Assessor — on tasks we all shared. From here, you point those same moves at one real child you just met.

2 · Field debrief — what do you understand now that you didn't before? (~35 min)

  • With a partner, then as a class: what surprised you in your placement? What was different from what you expected a classroom to be?
  • What did your host teacher point to — a topic that's coming, a group that's stuck, something they wish they had more time for?
  • What did you see your student do with mathematics? Describe it without judging it yet.

Purpose: the debrief is where the field stops being a blur and becomes specific. A research question can only grow out of something specific you noticed.

3 · Back to your student (~20 min)

  • Return to the learner you identified in the field — your Student Profile and the work you collected. Remind yourself of the mathematics you're setting out to understand and where it's headed for them.
  • Look at the samples again, with fresh eyes. What do you notice now that you couldn't in the moment?
  • Start a running list of questions and wonderings about how that child is thinking. Don't polish them — you'll work on them Thursday. Keep the list.
  • If you couldn't settle on one student in the field, or your placement changed, flag it now so we can sort it out before Thursday.

Purpose: the choice was made in the field — today you reconnect with that student's work and start the raw questions Thursday's protocol will sharpen.

4 · Questions about our project (~15 min)

  • Here is where the debrief is heading: the question you build becomes the spine of the whole back half of the course — the lesson you'll design over the next few weeks (see the arc above) exists to investigate it.
  • So a good question is one that lesson could actually help you investigate. We open the Research Question assignment together and read how the pieces fit.
  • What do you still want to know about how this project works? Ask now.

Purpose: knowing the question has to be answerable by one lesson's evidence keeps it grounded — investigable with what you'll actually build and teach.

Wrap-up & looking ahead:
  • Before Thursday: open the Research Question Studio and try it — load your learner, add a piece of the work you collected, and see how it asks you to look. Bring the questions and wonderings from today.
  • Thursday is a working session: you'll turn that raw list into one research question, then hand it to a genAI partner to sharpen — not to write.
