Question-Strengthening

You can already spot a strong research question. Today: how to make yours stronger — one move at a time.

Move 1 · Before
  • K · countingCan Robin count to 20?
  • Gr 3 · factsDoes Kai know 6 × 7?
  • Gr 5 · decimalsCan Sam order decimals correctly?
What do these three have in common — and what would make each a stronger research question?
Move 1 · After
  • Can Robin count to 20?
    When Robin counts a group of objects, how does Robin keep track of which are already counted — and what happens when I rearrange them?
  • Does Kai know 6 × 7?
    When Kai meets a multiplication fact they don't know by heart, how do they figure it out?
  • Can Sam order decimals correctly?
    How does Sam decide which of 0.45 and 0.5 is larger — what are they attending to?
The move: from did they get it right → how do they make sense of it.
Move 2 · Before
  • Gr 4 · fractionsHow does my student understand fractions?
  • Gr 2 · additionHow does Ari understand addition?
  • Gr 1 · geometryHow does Lee understand geometry?
These are already about understanding — so what's still too big to investigate?
Move 2 · After
  • How does my student understand fractions?
    How does Jordan treat a fraction like 3/4 as a single number — for instance, when placing it on a number line from 0 to 1?
  • How does Ari understand addition?
    When Ari adds two two-digit numbers, how do they handle the tens and the ones — keep place value, or count all?
  • How does Lee understand geometry?
    How does Lee decide whether a shape is a triangle when it's turned or stretched?
The move: narrow a whole domain to one facet a single task can surface.
Move 3 · Before
  • Gr 3 · subtractionWhy can't Alex regroup when subtracting?
  • Gr 5 · fractionsWhy does Nico struggle to add unlike fractions?
  • Gr 2 · word problemsWhy can't Dana solve word problems?
What does each of these assume before we've even looked?
Move 3 · After
  • Why can't Alex regroup when subtracting?
    When a subtraction needs regrouping, what does Alex actually do — and what does that reveal about how they think about tens and ones?
  • Why does Nico struggle to add unlike fractions?
    When Nico adds fractions with different denominators, what rule are they following — and what does it show about how they see the pieces?
  • Why can't Dana solve word problems?
    When Dana meets a word problem, how do they decide which operation to use — what in the problem are they keying on?
The move: from why can't they / what's missing → what is their current, sensible logic.
Move 4 · Before
  • Gr 2 · place valueDoes Sam understand place value?
  • Gr 4 · multiplicationHow does Rae think about multiplication?
  • K · numberDoes Robin know their numbers?
Each of these could be about any child. What would tie it to this one?
Move 4 · After
  • Does Sam understand place value?
    In her work, Sam decided 45 > 63 “because 5 is more than 3.” How is Sam using the digits to decide which number is larger?
  • How does Rae think about multiplication?
    Rae solved 4 × 16 as 4 × 10 and 4 × 6, then added. How does Rae use place value to break multiplication apart — and does it hold with harder numbers?
  • Does Robin know their numbers?
    Robin said “six” instantly for a die's dots but counted a scattered set of six. How does the arrangement change how Robin sees a quantity?
The move: ground it in something this child actually did.
Move 5 · Before
  • Gr 3 · a year of growthHow has my student improved in math this year?
  • Gr 5 · fractionsDoes Nico understand all of fractions?
  • Gr 1 · number senseHow is Lee's number sense developing?
Could one lesson's worth of student work actually answer these?
Move 5 · After
  • How has my student improved in math this year?
    What does Alex attend to when deciding which of two fractions is larger?
  • Does Nico understand all of fractions?
    When Nico compares 2/3 and 3/4, what strategy do they reach for?
  • How is Lee's number sense developing?
    When Lee adds within 20, do they count on from the larger number or count all — and when do they switch?
The move: right-size it — to what one lesson's work could actually show.
